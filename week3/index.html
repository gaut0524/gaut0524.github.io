Jackie (CEO)

About Me

I was born and raised in Hong Kong, where I was caught up in the hustle and bustle of the city. I picked my career accordingly, but something always felt amiss. I have always dreamed of owning a coffee shop, but I had to give up that dream in order to live comfortably in my city. But now that I've moved to Canada and have established myself in the business world, I want to finally pursue my childhood dreams.

I hardly consider this a job; this is more like a passion project. When I think about it, I realize how fortunate I am to be brewing coffee, tea, wonderful, caffeinated concoctions, and delicate, aromatic teas. It also doesn't hurt to be running a business alongside my daughter. She bakes and I brew, and it's a great system if I do say so myself.

What's my favourite quote? Well, it'd have to be:
"Nothing is impossible, the word itself says I'm possible." - Audrey Hepburn

Anita (head baker)

About Me

Like my mother, I was born in Hong Kong too. I was raised there until the age of 11, when I moved here. I met some family members who were passionate about food after relocating to Ottawa. From there, I picked up some simple baking skills and occasionally assisted them in the kitchen. Even yet, my love for baking was still in its infancy. It wasn't until my 17th year that I started to experiment with recipes and cook on my own. Once I started my culinary journey, I couldn't stop. I frequently prepared meals for my family, friends, and neighbours, and I realized baking held a special place in my heart.

When my mother sat me down and told me about her coffee-shop dream I was taken aback. She had never expressed any interest, much less talked about this to any degree. But then she asked me to be her baker, and without so much as a second thought I replied yes. Thus, our journey began. Like any business we encountered challenges, but I wouldn't trade any of it for the world. I spend all day doing what I love, surrounded by the people I love.

My favourite part of the job would have to be the customers. Old and young, new and regularâ€”through serving them, we catch a glimpse into their lives. I consider our store a harbour for people from all walks of life. And ultimately, as long as my coffee shop can bring them a modicum of joy or relief, then I'd say my job is well worth it.

If I had to pick my favourite quote, I'd choose:
"Faithless is he who says farewell when the road darkens." - J.R.R Tolkien, The Fellowship of the Ring
